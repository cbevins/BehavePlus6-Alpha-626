Diameter of Viable Embers at Surface

Diameter of viable embers at surface is used in SPOT to calculate spotting distance for an active crown fire. It is the diameter of an ember that reaches the ground and still has enough energy to ignite surrounding fuel. The smaller the viable ember, the longer the maximum spotting distance. The model assumes the smallest viable ember is 0.5 mm.
