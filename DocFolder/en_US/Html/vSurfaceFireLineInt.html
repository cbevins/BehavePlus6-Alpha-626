Surface Fireline Intensity

Surface fireline intensity is the heat energy release per unit time from a one-foot (one-meter) wide section of the fuel bed extending from the front to the rear of the flaming zone of a surface fire. Surface fireline intensity is a function of surface fire rate of spread and surface fire heat per unit area.

Both surface fireline intensity and surface fire flame length are related to the heat felt by a person standing next to the flames. Surface fireline intensity is directly related to and is used in the calculation of flame length.

For more information on the effect of fire spread direction on surface fireline intensity, see Fire Spread Directions.

I/O Module If Notes
Input CROWN If SURFACE is not selected and if both Crown fire is calculated using Rothermel (1991) and Surface fire intensity is entered as flame length are selected as input options. If SURFACE and CROWN are both selected, the surface fireline intensity (flame length) in the direction of fire spread is calculated in SURFACE and used by CROWN.
SCORCH If SURFACE is not selected and if Surface fire intensity is entered as fireline intensity is selected as an input option. If SURFACE and SCORCH are both selected, fireline intensity (flame length) calculated in SURFACE is used by SCORCH for the specified direction of spread.
Output SURFACE    

The flaming zone, or flaming segment, is the area for which surface fireline intensity and surface fire flame length are calculated.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from IGNITION POINT is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment perpendicular to the perimeter. This is the improved method, added in version 6.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from FIRE PERIMETER is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment in the specified direction of fire spread.
