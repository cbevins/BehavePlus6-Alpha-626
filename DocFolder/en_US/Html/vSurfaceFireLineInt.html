Surface Fireline Intensity

Surface fireline intensity is the heat energy release per unit time from a one-foot (one-meter) wide section of the fuel bed extending from the front to the rear of the flaming zone of a surface fire. Fireline intensity is a function of rate of spread and heat per unit area, and is directly related to flame length.

Fireline intensity and flame length are related to the heat felt by a person standing next to the flames.

For more information on fire spread direction options, see Fire Spread Directions.

The flaming zone, or flaming segment, is the area for which fireline intensity and flame length are calculated.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from IGNITON POINT. (FL based on flaming segment perpendicular to perimeter, improved V6 method) is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment perpendicular to the perimeter. This is the improved method, added in version 6.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from Ignition Point. (FL based on flaming segment in direction from igntion point, method through V5) is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment in the direction from the ignition point. This is the method through version 5 and is included only for comparison and continuity.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from FIRE FRONT. is selected, the fire is a point source fire. The fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment in the direction of fire spread.
