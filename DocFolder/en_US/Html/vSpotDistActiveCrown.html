Spotting Distance from Active Crown Fire

Spotting distance from an active crown fire is the maximum distance that one can expect potential spot fires resulting from firebrands from an active crown fire to occur. The crown fire is assumed to occur in a conifer forest type. This model does not consider whether a firebrand will cause a spot fire to occur as it does not calculate the probability of ignition.

The spotting model is applicable under conditions of active or running crown fire under which spotting distance of up to several miles might be expected. This model is not suitable for less extreme fire conditions such as torching trees.

This model also does not consider spotting associated with fire whirls or unusual fire-induced phenomena. It is not approproate for spotting distances in areas such as eucalypt forests because of the unique bark characteristics associated with such trees.

I/O Module If Notes

Input

None

 

  

Output

SPOT
