Flame Residence Time

Flame residence time is a function of the characteristic surface area-to-volume ratio of the fuel bed as calculated by Rothermel's (1972) surface fire spread model. It is used to calculate flame depth and surface fire heat per unit area. Surface fireline intensity is calculated as the product of reaction intensity times flame depth or the surface fire heat per unit area times surface fire rate of spread.

Flame residence time only applies to the fine fuels that carry fire spread. Flames are also a result of fuels burning behind the fire front; this is not considered in the model. Therefore, care should be taken in interpreting and using flame residence time.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SURFACE   Intermediate value
