Heat Source

Heat source is an intermediate value in Rothermel's (1972) surface fire spread model. It is the heat flux received from the source and is dependent upon both the reaction intensity and the propagating flux ratio. This heat source value is then increased by wind and slope factors that affect flame tilt over the potential fuel to get the total propagating heat flux or heat source. The surface rate of spread is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, the denominator).
