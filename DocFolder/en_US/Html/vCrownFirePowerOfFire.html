Power of the Fire

Power of the fire is described by Rothermel (1991) and is based on work by Byram (1959). Power of the fire is the rate at which energy is released by the fire, calculated on a unit area basis. This power is the source of energy that produces the convection column. If the power of the fire is greater than the power of the wind, then the fire-wind system may be dominated by the energy of the fire. Such active crown fires may be plume-dominated and stand almost vertically. We cannot yet predict the spread rate of a plume-dominated fire.

Fire Type outputs are only valid for the Rothermel (1991) crown fire method.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output CROWN If Crown fire is calculated using Rothermel (1991) is selected as an input option.
