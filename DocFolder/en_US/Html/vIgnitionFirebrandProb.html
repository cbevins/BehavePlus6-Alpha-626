Probability of Ignition from a Firebrand

Probability of ignition from a firebrand is an indication of the chance that a firebrand will cause an ignition. The number of firebrands, their size, and the fuel on which they land is generally unknown. Therefore, there is no specific interpretation of the probability of ignition of a fire (Schroeder, 1969).

The calculation of probability of ignition is based on fuel shading from the sun, fine dead (1-h) fuel moisture, and the air temperature.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output IGNITE    

Probability of ignition from a firebrand should not be confused with the Ignition Component from the National Fire Danger Rating System (Bradshaw and others 1983).
