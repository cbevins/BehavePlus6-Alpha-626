Crown Fire Length-to-Width Ratio

The crown fire length-to-width ratio is used to describe an elliptically shaped fire. For crown fire, it is based on 20-foot wind speed and is used to calculate crown fire perimeter as described by Rothermel (1991, p. 16). A similar calculation for surface fire length-to-width ratio is used to calculate surface fire perimeter using the effective wind speed (combined estimated effect of wind and slope). Slope is not factored into the crown fire length-to-width ratio. In both cases, the calculated fire shape becomes longer and narrower as the length-to-width ratio increases.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output CROWN
