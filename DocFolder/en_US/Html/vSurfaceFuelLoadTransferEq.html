Fuel Model Type

Fuel model type is static (S) or dynamic (D) for standard or custom fuel models. Dynamic fuel models have a live herbaceous fuel component, a portion of which is transferred into the dead herbaceous fuel load depending upon the live herbaceous fuel moisture content; the lower the moisture content, the greater the load transfer. The fuel load transfer portion can also be entered directly. If calculated, fuel load transfer only occurs when the herbaceous live fuel moisture ranges from 30% to 120%.

17 of the standard fuel models created by Scott and Burgan (2005) are dynamic. All other fuel models described by Anderson (1993) and Scott and Burgan (2005) are static. Dynamic fuel models can be identified in several ways.

  • In the Input Guide window, there is a "(D)" after every dynamic fuel model, and an "(S)" after every static fuel model.
  • The fuel model parameters for the dynamic fuel models contain live herbaceous fuel. Note, however, that the original 13 fuel models are all static models even if they contain live herbaceous fuel.
  • The publication by Scott and Burgan (2005) includes the word "(dynamic)" in the title for the fuel model description. Static fuel models are not labeled as such.

The fuel load transfer equation is:

  • fraction = 1.333 - (0.0111 × moisture)

where fraction is the fraction of live fuel load transferred from live herbaceous to the dead herbaceous fuel category, and moisture is the live herbaceous fuel moisture content (%). The following table shows some representative values (Scott and Burgan 2005).
