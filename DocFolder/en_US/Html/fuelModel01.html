Fire Behavior Fuel Model 1 -- Short Grass

Anderson (1982) classifies Fire Behavior Fuel Model 1 in the Grass Fuel Type and describes it as follows:

Fire spread is governed by the fine, very porous, and continuous herbaceous fuels that have cured or are nearly cured. Fires are surface fires that move rapidly through the cured grass and associated material. Very little shrub or timber is present, generally less than one-third of the area. Grasslands and savanna are represented along with stubble, grass-tundra, and grass-shrub combinations that met the above area constraint. Annual and perennial grasses are included in this fuel model.

Fuel model parameters are available by viewing the Fuel Model Table of Parameters or right-clicking on the fuel model description in the Input Guide window and selecting View parameters.
