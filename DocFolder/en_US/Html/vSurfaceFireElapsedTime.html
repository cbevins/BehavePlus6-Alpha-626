Elapsed Time

Elapsed time is the time for which the fire is spreading at an assumed constant rate. Fire rate of spread is multiplied by elapsed time to calculate the spread distance of a surface fire or a crown fire. It is required when any of the spread distance outputs are requested.

  • For SURFACE, the fuel, fuel moisture, wind, and slope are assumed to be constant for the entire elapsed time. Because wildland fires burn under non-uniform conditions, the elapsed time and choice of input values must be considered carefully to achieve useful projections.
  • The fire model in CROWN assumes fire spread times on the order of hours. During that time conditions can vary considerably, so caution should be used.
  • In SIZE, elapsed time is the time from when a fire begins spreading (which may be different from the time of ignition) until the time that the size calculation is done.
