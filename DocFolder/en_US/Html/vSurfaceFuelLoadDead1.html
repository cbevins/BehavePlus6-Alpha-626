1-h Fuel Load

1-h fuel load is the weight of the 1-h dead fuel per unit area, and is a fuel model parameter. The one-hour (1-h) timelag dead fuel category includes dead fuel from 0 to 0.25 inches (0 to 0.64 cm) in diameter. This includes needles, leaves, cured herbaceous plants and fine dead stems of plants. This is an important fuel parameter for Rothermel's (1972) fire spread model.
