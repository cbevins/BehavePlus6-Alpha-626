Heat Sink

Heat sink is an intermediate value in Rothermel's (1972) surface fire spread model. The heat required for ignition is dependent upon ignition temperature, moisture content of the fuel, and the amount of fuel involved in the ignition process.

Surface fire rate of spread is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, the denominator).

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SURFACE   Intermediate value
