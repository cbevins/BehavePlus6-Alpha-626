Downwind Canopy Cover

Downwind canopy cover is the percent of the ground covered by overstory canopy downwind of a spot fire source (in the direction firebrands are carried). It is used to determine if the canopy downwind is Open or Closed. If downwind canopy cover is Open, the downwind canopy height will be divided in half before calculating spotting distance from torching trees, burning piles, and wind-driven surface fires.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SPOT If Spotting Distance from Torching Trees, Spotting Distance from a Burning Pile, or Spotting Distance from a Wind-Driven Surface Fire is selected as an output variable.  
Output None
