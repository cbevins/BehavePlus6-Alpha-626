SURFACE Directions Input Options

This page controls how the SURFACE Module treats wind and spread direction inputs.

Options

Surface fire spread direction is....
  HEADING only (in direction of maximum spread). Only the head fire rate of spread is calculated. The direction of maximum spread is determined by slope and wind directions.
Output includes a heading line: 'Head Fire'.
FLANKING only (90 deg from head fire). Only the flanking fire rate of spread is calculated. The direction from the fire front is 90 degrees from the direction of maximum spread (not 90 degrees from the ignition point).
Output includes a heading line: 'Flanking Fire'.
See Fire Spread Directions for more information.
BACKING only (180 deg from head fire). Only the backing fire rate of spread is calculated. The direction is 180 degrees from the direction of maximum spread.
Output includes a heading line: 'Backing Fire'.
Directions from FIRE FRONT. Spread rate is calculated for the directions from the fire front as specified on the worksheet. Spread direction is normal (perpendicular) to the perimeter. This option can apply to either point source or line source fire.
Output includes a heading line: 'Spread from Fire Front'.
See Fire Spread Directions for more information.
Directions from IGNITION POINT. (FL based on flaming segment perpendicular to perimeter, improved V6 method) Spread rate is calculated for the directions from the ignition point of a point source elliptically shaped fire as specified on the worksheet. An improved method is used for calculating fireline intensity and flame length in directions other than head and back.
Output includes a heading line: 'Spread from Ignition Point'.
See Fire Spread Directions for more information.
Directions from Ignition Point. (FL based on flaming segment in direction from ignition point, method through V5) Spread rate is calculated for the directions from the ignition point of a point source fire as specified on the worksheet. This method of calculating fireline intensity and flame length is included only to provide a means of comparing the improved V6 method of calculating flame length in directions other than head or backing to the old V5 method.
Output includes a heading line: 'Spread from Ignition Point, V5 Directional FL Calculation'.
See Fire Spread Directions for more information.
 
Wind & spread directions are....
  degrees clockwise from upslope (direction the wind is pushing the fire).

Wind direction is the direction the wind is pushing the fire relative to the upslope direction (NOT the direction from which the wind is blowing). This facilitates visualization of vectoring methods.

  • Enter 0 degrees for an upslope wind.
  • Enter 180 degrees for a downslope wind.
  • Enter 90 degrees for a cross-slope wind blowing "left-to-right".
  • Enter 270 degrees for a cross-slope wind blowing "right-to-left".
degrees clockwise from north (direction from which the wind is blowing).

Wind direction is the direction from which the wind is blowing in a traditional sense. This selection also requires input of aspect.

For example:

  • Enter 0 degrees for a North Wind (the wind is blowing from the north and is pushing the fire to the south).
  • Enter 90 degrees for an East Wind (the wind is blowing from the east and is pushing the fire to the west).
  • Enter 180 degrees for a South Wind (the wind is blowing from the south and is pushing the fire to the north).
  • Enter 270 degrees for a West Wind (the wind is blowing from the west and is pushing the fire to the east).
