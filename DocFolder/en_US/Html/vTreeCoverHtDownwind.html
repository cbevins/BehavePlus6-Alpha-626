Downwind Canopy Height

Downwind canopy height is used to calculate spotting distance in SPOT. It is the mean cover height in the downwind direction as it affects the distance a firebrand is carried. In v5.0 and earlier, the downwind canopy height needed to be divided in half for open canopies. In v6.0, that correction is made by setting the downwind canopy cover to Open.

The value for canopy height, which is used to calculate wind adjustment factor, may be different than downwind canopy height. Canopy height represents the fire site for calculation of wind adjustment factor, crown fireline intensity, and tree mortality. For example, the canopy height at the site of the fire may be zero (no overstory) while the downwind canopy height (in the direction firebrands are carried) may be 75 feet (22.9 m).
