Fuel Shading from the Sun

Fuel shading from the sun is the portion of the fuel that is not receiving direct sunlight due to the combination of cloud cover and overstory. It is used to calculate probability of ignition by a firebrand.

Less than 50% shading is sometimes referred to an unshaded condition, while 50% or more shading is referred to as a shaded condition.

I/O Module If Notes
Input IGNITE    
Output None
