Fuel Shading from the Sun

Fuel shading from the sun is the portion of the fuel that is not receiving direct sunlight due to cloud cover and overstory. It is used to calculate probability of ignition by a firebrand.

Less than 50% shading is sometimes referred to as being in an unshaded condition; 50% or more shading is referred to as a shaded condition.
