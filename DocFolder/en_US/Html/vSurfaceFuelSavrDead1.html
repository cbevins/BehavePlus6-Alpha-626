1-h Fuel Surface Area-to-Volume Ratio

1-h fuel surface area-to-volume ratio (SA/V) is the amount of area on the outside of the fuel (surface area) divided by the volume of that fuel. 1-h fuel is dead fuel that is 0 to 0.25 in (0 to 0.64 cm) diameter. The 1-h fuel SA/V is a fuel model parameter.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SURFACE If Fuel is entered as fuel parameters is selected as an input option. If Fuel is entered as fuel models then the value stored for the standard or custom fuel model is used.
Output SURFACE If Fuel model is entered as special case fuel model, western aspen is selected as an input option.  

A way to visualize the surface area-to-volume ratio is to think about the square feet of wrapping paper needed to wrap a box divided by the volume of the box in cubic feet.

Estimating surface area-to-volume ratio
  • Some coarse grasses have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 500 to 1,500 ft2 / ft3 (1,640 to 4,921 m2 / m3).
  • Average grasses have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 1,500 to 2,500 ft2 / ft3 (4,921 to 8,202 m2 / m3).
  • Fine grasses have 1-h fuel SA/V of 2,500+ ft2 / ft3 (8,202 m2 / m3).
