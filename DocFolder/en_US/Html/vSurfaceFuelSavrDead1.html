1-h Surface Area-to-Volume Ratio

1-h surface area-to-volume ratio (SA/V) is the amount of area on the outside of the fuel (surface area) divided by the volume of the fuel. 1-h fuel is dead fuel 0 to 0.25 in (0 to 0.64 cm) diameter. The 1-h SA/V is a fuel model parameter.

I/O

Module

If

Notes
Input SURFACE If Fuel is entered as fuel parameters is selected as an input option. If Fuel is entered as fuel models then the value stored for the standard or custom fuel model is used.
Output SURFACE If Fuel model is entered as special case fuel model, western aspen is selected as an input option.  

A way to visualize the surface area-to-volume ratio is the square feet of wrapping paper needed to wrap a box divided by the volume of the box in cubic feet.

Some coarse grasses and some conifer needles have a 1-h SA/V of 500 to 1,500 ft2 / ft3 (1,640 to 4,921 m2 / m3).

Average grasses, some broadleaf tree leaves, and most conifer needles have a 1-h SA/V of 1,500 to 2,500 ft2 / ft3 (4,921 to 8,202 m2 / m3).

Fine grasses, most broadleaf tree leaves, and some conifer needles have 1-h SA/V of 2,500+ ft2 / ft3 (8,202 m2 / m3).

The 10-h and 100-h SA/V are set at 109 ft2 / ft3 (358 m2 / m3) and 30 ft2 / ft3 (98 m2 / m3) for all fire behavior fuel models.
