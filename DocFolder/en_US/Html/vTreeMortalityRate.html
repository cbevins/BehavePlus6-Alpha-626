Probability of Mortality

Probability of mortality is the likelihood that a tree will be killed by a fire. The equation used to calculate probability of mortality depends on the mortality tree species. There are 13 mortality equations, which variously include bark thickness, tree crown length scorched and/or tree crown volume scorched.

Probability of mortality can be interpreted as the probability of an individual tree dying, or it can be multiplied by the number of trees on a site to estimate the number of trees that will die.

Probability of aspen mortality for western aspen is calculated differently and is available by selecting the western aspen special case fuel model in SURFACE.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output MORTALITY
