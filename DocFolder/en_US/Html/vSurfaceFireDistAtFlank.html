Surface Fire Flanking Spread Distance

The surface fire flanking spread distance of a point source fire is based on the elliptical fire shape model and is the distance the surface fire travels in a specified period of time. The direction from the fire perimeter is 90 degrees from the direction of maximum spread. The distance to the perimeter is half the maximum fire width.

For more information on fire spread direction options, see Fire Spread Directions.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SIZE   Can also be output as a map distance if Display output distances in map units is checked.
