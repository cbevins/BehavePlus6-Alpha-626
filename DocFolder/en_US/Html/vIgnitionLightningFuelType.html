Lightning Ignition Fuel Type

Lightning ignition fuel type is used to calculate probability of ignition from a lightning strike. There are eight fuel types, which are only used for this specific ignition model. The research paper by Latham and Schlieter (1989) lists additional tree species that may be modeled based on these eight fuel types.

Depending on fuel type, ignition from lightning is a function of either 100-h moisture content or lightning duff and litter depth.

Application of these models to other species is based on similarity as documented in Agriculture Handbook 519 (Little 1978) and after consultation with Dr. James K. Brown of the Intermountain Fire Sciences Laboratory (now the Missoula Fire Sciences Laboratory home of the Fire, Fuel, and Smoke Science Program). Expert judgment should be taken when using the models for the species listed below as independent tests of ignition probability were not performed for each species.
