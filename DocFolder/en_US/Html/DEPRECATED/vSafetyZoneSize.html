Circular Safety Zone Size

Circular safety zone size is the area of a circular safety zone just large enough to protect the specified number of personnel and heavy equipment from radiant burn injury. Calculation is based on the safety zone radius, a combination of radius of the Safe Area and the Separation Distance. The Safe Area is a circle based on the calculation of the area required for all personnel and equipment. The separation distance is based on flame height. Flame length is used as a worst-case estimate of flame height and can be calculated for a surface fire in the SURFACE module. Flame height must be entered directly by the user for a crown fire. Although crown fire flame length can be calculated in the CROWN module, there is not a link to SAFETY.

The area of a circle is the smallest area that provides the required separation distance. A non-circular safety zone (such as a square) that provides the required protection on all sides will have a greater area than calculated for the circular safety zone.

Existing or natural safety zones (such as rockslides, barren areas, and some recently burned areas) are rarely circular or square. To be effective, a circle of the specified radius must fit within the existing safety zone.
