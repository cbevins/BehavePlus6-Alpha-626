1-h Fuel Moisture

1-h fuel moisture content is the portion (percent or fraction) of the 1-h timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis. This value is also used for dead herbaceous fuel moisture content for dynamic fuel models. The 1-h fuel moisture content has more influence on the fire spread calculations than does the 100-h fuel moisture content.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SURFACE    
IGNITE If Probability of Ignition from a Firebrand is selected as output variable.  
CROWN    
Output None    

The surface fire model uses a weighting process to combine the 1-h, 10-h and 100-h fuel moistures into a characteristic (weighted average) dead fuel moisture. Because the weighting is based on surface area-to-volume ratio, the 1-h fuel moisture is the predominant driving variable for surface fire spread calculations.

Timelag is a term used to refer to the amount of time it takes a dead fuel particle to reach about 63% of the difference between its current moisture content and the equilibrium moisture content.
