Passive Crown Fire Rate of Spread

The passive crown fire rate of spread is the head fire (forward) spread rate of a passive crown fire. It is the estimated rate of spread as a fire transitions from a surface fire to a crown fire. Both surface and active crown fire rates of spread are calculated. The crown fraction burned (Van Wagner 1993; Scott and Reinhardt 2001), ranging from 0 for surface fire to 1 for a fully active crown fire, is then used as a transition term within the model. The resulting passive crown fire rate of spread falls somewhere between surface fire rate of spread and crown fire rate of spread.

Passive crown fire outputs are only valid for the Scott and Reinhardt (2001) crown fire method.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output CROWN If Crown fire is calculated using Scott and Reinhardt (2001) is selected as an input option.
