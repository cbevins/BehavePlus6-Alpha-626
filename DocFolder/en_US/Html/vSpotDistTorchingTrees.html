Spotting Distance from Torching Trees

Spotting distance from torching trees is the maximum distance that one can expect potential spot fires to result from firebrands from torching trees. Most spot fires will be much closer to the fire.

The spotting model for torching trees is applicable under conditions of intermediate fire severity in which spotting distance up to a mile or two might be expected. This model does not apply to extreme situations where spotting may occur up to tens of miles from the fire perimeter. The model is for spotting from torching trees, not from an active, spreading crown fire.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SPOT   Can also be output as a map distance if Display output distances in map units is checked.
