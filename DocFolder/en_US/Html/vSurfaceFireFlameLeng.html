Surface Fire Flame Length

Surface fire flame length of a spreading surface fire within the flaming front is measured from midway in the active flaming combustion zone to the average tip of the flames. According to Rothermel (1991), "Flame length is an elusive parameter that exists in the eye of the beholder. It is a poor quantity to use in a scientific or engineering sense, but it is so readily apparent to fireline personnel and so readily conveys a sense of fire intensity that it is worth featuring as a primary fire variable."

Both surface fireline intensity and surface fire flame length are related to the heat felt by a person standing next to the flames. Flame length is calculated from fireline intensity, which is a function of surface fire rate of spread and heat per unit area.

For more information on the effect of surface fire spread direction on surface fire flame length, see Fire Spread Directions.

I/O Module If Notes
Input CROWN If SURFACE is not selected and if both Crown fire is calculated using Rothermel (1991) and Surface fire intensity is entered as flame length are selected as input options. If SURFACE and CROWN are both selected, the flame length in the direction of fire spread is calculated in SURFACE and used by CROWN.
SCORCH If SURFACE is not selected and if Fire intensity is entered as flame length is selected as an input option. If SURFACE and SCORCH are both selected, the flame length in the direction of fire spread is calculated in SURFACE and used by SCORCH.
SPOT If SURFACE is not selected and spotting distance from a wind-driven surface fire is selected as an output variable. If SURFACE and SPOT are both selected, the flame length must be for the head fire, in the direction of maximum spread.
Output SURFACE    

The flaming zone, or flaming segment, is the area for which fireline intensity and flame length are calculated.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from IGNITION POINT is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment perpendicular to the perimeter. This is the improved method, added in version 6.

When Surface fire spread is Directions from FIRE PERIMETER is selected, the fireline intensity and flame length are based on the flaming segment in the specified direction of fire spread.
