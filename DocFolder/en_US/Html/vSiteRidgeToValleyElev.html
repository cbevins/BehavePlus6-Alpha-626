Ridge-to-Valley Elevation Difference

Ridge-to-valley elevation difference is the vertical elevation difference in feet or meters between ridge top and valley bottom in the direction of terrain flow. A stylized terrain shape is used by the model to calculate spotting distance.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SPOT    
Output None
