Characteristic Surface Area-to-Volume Ratio

Surface area-to-volume ratio is the area on the outside of the fuel (surface area) divided by the volume of the fuel. The fuel bed characteristic surface area-to-volume ratio (sigma, ft3 / ft2) is a weighted value for all dead and live fuel classes combined. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's (1972) surface fire spread model.

I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SURFACE   Intermediate value

A way to visualize the surface area-to-volume ratio is to think about the square feet of wrapping paper needed to wrap a box divided by the volume of the box in cubic feet.

Estimating surface area-to-volume ratio
  • Some coarse grasses and some conifer needles have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 500 to 1,500 square feet per cubic foot (1,640 to 4,921 m2 / m3).
  • Average grasses, broadleaf tree leaves, and most conifer needles have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 1,500 to 2,500 square feet per cubic foot (4,921 to 8,202 m2 / m3).
  • Fine grasses, most broadleaf tree leaves, and some conifer needles have 1-h fuel SA/V of 2,500+ square feet per cubic foot (8,202+ m2 / m3).
  • The 10-h fuel SA/V is set to 109 ft2 / ft3 (358 m2 / m3) for all standard fire behavior fuel models.
  • The 100-h fuel SA/V is set to 30 ft2 / ft3 (98 m2 / m3) for all standard fire behavior fuel models.
