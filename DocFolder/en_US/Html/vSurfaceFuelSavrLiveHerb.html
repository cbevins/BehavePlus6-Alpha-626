Live Herbaceous Fuel Surface Area-to-Volume Ratio

Live herbaceous fuel surface area-to-volume ratio (SA/V) is the total surface area of a herbaceous plant leaf divided by the volume occupied by that plant leaf. Live herbaceous fuel surface area-to-volume ratio is a fuel model parameter.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SURFACE If Fuel is entered as fuel parameters is selected as an input option. If Fuel is entered as fuel models then the value stored for the standard or custom fuel model is used.
Output None    

A way to visualize the surface area-to-volume ratio is to think about the square feet of wrapping paper needed to wrap a box divided by the volume of the box in cubic feet.

Estimating surface area-to-volume ratio
  • Some conifer needles have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 500 to 1,500 square feet per cubic foot (1,640 to 4,921 m2 / m3).
  • Average broadleaf tree leaves, and most conifer needles have a 1-h fuel SA/V of 1,500 to 2,500 square feet per cubic foot (4,921 to 8,202 m2 / m3).
  • Most broadleaf tree leaves, and some conifer needles have 1-h fuel SA/V of 2,500+ square feet per cubic foot (8,202 m2 / m3).
