Surface Fire Spread Distance

Surface fire spread distance is the distance a surface fire travels in a specified direction during a specified elapsed time. It is the product of the surface fire rate of spread and elapsed time.

See Surface Fire Rate of Spread.

Under certain conditions, surface fire spread distance is the same as spread distances calculated in the SIZE module.

  • If a heading fire is modeled, and Surface fire spread is...
    • HEADING only (in direction of maximum spread)
    • Directions from FIRE PERIMETER, and Surface Fire Spread Direction is heading ( direction of maximum spread)
    • Directions from IGNITION POINT, and Surface Fire Spread Direction is heading (direction of maximum spread)

  • If a backing fire (180 degrees from heading) is modeled, and Surface fire spread is...
    • BACKING only (180 degrees from head fire)
    • Directions from FIRE PERIMETER, and Surface Fire Spread Direction is backing (180 degrees from direction of maximum spread)
    • Directions from IGNITION POINT, and Surface Fire Spread Direction is backing (180 degrees from direction of maximum spread)

  • If a flanking fire (90 degrees from heading) is modeled, and Surface fire spread is...
    • FLANKING only (90 degrees from head fire)
    • Directions from FIRE PERIMETER, and Surface Fire Spread Direction is flanking (90 degrees from direction of maximum spread)


I/O Module If Notes
Input None    
Output SURFACE   Can also be output as a map distance if Display output distances in map units is checked.
