10-h Fuel Moisture

The 10-h fuel moisture content is the portion of the 10-h timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis. The 10-h fuel moisture has less influence on the fire spread calculations than does the 1-h fuel moisture. The fuel moisture obtained for the "fuel moisture sticks" at a National Fire Danger Rating System (NFDRS) weather station has sometimes been used as an estimate of 10-h fuel moisture, particularly in the past.

I/O Module If Notes
Input SURFACE If there is 10-h fuel in the fuel model.  
CROWN    
Output None    

The surface fire model uses a weighting process to combine the 1-h, 10-h and 100-h fuel moistures into a characteristic (weighted average) dead fuel moisture.

Timelag is a term used to refer to the amount of time it takes a dead fuel particle to reach about 63% of the difference between its current moisture content and the equilibrium moisture content.
