Ridge-to-Valley Horizontal Distance

The ridge-to-valley horizontal distance is the horizontal distance in miles or kilometers between the ridge top and the valley bottom in the direction of the wind flow. A stylized terrain shape is used by the model to calculate spotting distance.

If winds are blowing directly up a valley or down a valley, the ridge-to-valley horizontal distance most likely is zero.
