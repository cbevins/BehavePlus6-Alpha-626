SURFACE Directions Input Options

This page controls how the SURFACE Module treats wind and spread direction inputs.

Options

Surface fire spread direction is....
  only in the direction of maximum spread. Only the spread rate for the direction of maximum spread is calculated. It is determined by slope and wind direction.
in directions specified on the worksheet. Spread rate is calculated for the directions specified on the worksheet.
 
Wind is....
  on the worksheet. The wind direction is specified on the worksheet.
upslope. The wind is assumed to be blowing upslope, so wind direction is not required as an input. This is a simple, often used case. The head fire is upslope with the wind.
 
Wind & spread directions are....
  degrees clockwise from upslope (direction the wind is pushing the fire).

Wind direction is the direction the wind is pushing the fire relative to the upslope direction (NOT the direction from which the wind is blowing). This facilitates visualization of vectoring methods.

  • Enter 0 degrees for an upslope wind.
  • Enter 180 degrees for a downslope wind.
  • Enter 90 degrees for a cross-slope wind blowing "left-to-right".
  • Enter 270 degrees for a cross-slope wind blowing "right-to-left".
  degrees clockwise from north (direction from which the wind is blowing).

Wind direction is the direction from which the wind is blowing in a traditional sense. This selection also requires input of aspect.

For example:

  • Enter 0 degrees for a North Wind (the wind is blowing from the north and is pushing the fire to the south).
  • Enter 90 degrees for an East Wind (the wind is blowing from the east and is pushing the fire to the west).
  • Enter 180 degrees for a South Wind (the wind is blowing from the south and is pushing the fire to the north).
  • Enter 270 degrees for a West Wind (the wind is blowing from the west and is pushing the fire to the east).
