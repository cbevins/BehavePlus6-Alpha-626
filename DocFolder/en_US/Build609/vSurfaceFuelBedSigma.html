Characteristic Surface-Area-to-Volume Ratio

The surface-area-to-volume ratio is the area on the outside of the fuel (surface area) divided by the volume of the fuel. The fuel bed characteristic surface area-to-volume ratio (sigma, ft3 / ft2) is a weighted value for all dead and live fuel classes. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire spread model (1972).

Notes
Input None    
Output SURFACE   Intermediate value

A way to visualize the surface area-to-volume ratio is the square feet of wrapping paper needed to wrap a box divided by the volume of the box in cubic feet.

Some coarse grasses and some conifer needles have a 1-h SA/V of 500 to 1,500 square feet per cubic foot (1,640 to 4,921 m2 / m3).

Average grasses, broadleaf tree leaves, and most conifer needles have a 1-h SA/V of 1,500 to 2,500 square feet per cubic foot (4,921 to 8,202 m2 / m3).

Fine grasses, most broadleaf tree leaves, and some conifer needles have 1-h SA/V of 2,500+ square feet per cubic foot (8,202 m2 / m3).

The 10-h and 100-h SA/V are set at 109 ft2 / ft3 (358 m2 / m3) and 30 ft2 / ft3 (98 m2 / m3) for all fire behavior fuel models.
