Safety Zone Separation Distance

The safety zone separation distance is the minimum distance a firefighter in protective clothing must be separated from flames to prevent radiant heat injury. Separation distance is calculated as 4 x flame height. Flame length is used as a worst case estimator of flame height. If SURFACE is used to calculate flame length, the maximum flame length is used for SAFETY even if SURFACE calculations are for a direction other than for the direction of maximum spread.

To estimate separation distance from a crown fire, the user must enter the flame length. Crown fire flame length is calculated in CROWN, but there is not a link to SAFETY.

Separation distance is calculated to prevent only radiant heat injury; exposure to convective heat is not accounted for in this model.
