Fireline Intensity

Fireline intensity is the heat energy release per unit time from a one-foot (one-meter) wide section of the fuel bed extending from the front to the rear of the flaming zone. Fireline intensity is a function of rate of spread and heat per unit area, and is directly related to flame length.

Fireline intensity and the flame length are related to the heat felt by a person standing next to the flames.
