Tree Species Bark Thickness & Mortality Equations

Tree species is used in the BehavePlus MORTALITY and SPOT modules. There are, however, many more species available for MORTALITY than for SPOT. To avoid confusion, there are two tree species variables:

  • Spot tree species, and
  • Mortality tree species.
The same tree species codes are used for both variables. They have been updaated to match the codes used in FOFEM 6.0. The table below lists all tree species available to the MORTALITY module. The tree species available to the SPOT module are listed in
  • Spot Tree Species

Bark thickness is estimated from the tree species and D.B.H. using equations cited in FOFEM 6.0 User Guide (Lutes, Duncan C., 2013).

Tree mortality equations 1, 3 are from the following journal article: Ryan, K.C.; Reinhardt, E.D. 1988. Predicting post fire mortality of seven western conifers. Canadian Journal of Forest Research 18:1291-1297.

Tree mortality equation 5 is a longleaf pine mortality equation, which, along with the associated bark thickness equation, came from JFSP Report #05-4-3-06 Modify FOFEM for use in the Coastal Plain Region of the Southeastern U.S. (Wang, G.G.; Wangen, S.; Reinhardt, E.; Waldrop, T.A., Outcalt, K.W.; Walker, J.L.; Brockway, D.G.; Haywood, J.D.; Hiers, J.K.. 2007).

Tree mortality equations 10-20 are from JFSP Report #05-2-1-105 Delayed Tree Mortality following Fire in Western Conifers (Hood, S.;Smith, S.; Cluck, D.; Reinhardt, E.; Ryan, K., 2008), representing "pre-fire" estimates of mortality.

Each tree species has a bark thickness equation and a mortality equation associated with it. These are the same equations as in FOFEM 6.0 and are summarized in the tables below.

Pm = Probability of Mortality (fraction); CLS = Crown Length Scorched (%); CVS = Crown Volume Scorched (%)
For Equations 1 and 3: BT = Bark Thickness (inches); DBH = Diameter at Breast Height (inches)
For Equations 5, 14, and 17: BT = Bark Thickness (cm); DBH = Diameter at Breast Height (cm)
