Spotting Distance from Torching Trees

Spotting distance from torching trees is the maximum distance that one can expect potential spot fires resulting from firebrands from torching trees.

The spotting model is applicable under conditions of intermediate fire severity in which spotting distance up to a mile or two might be expected. This model does not apply to those extreme situations where spotting may occur up to tens of miles from the fire front. The model is for spotting from torching trees, not from a spreading crown fire.
