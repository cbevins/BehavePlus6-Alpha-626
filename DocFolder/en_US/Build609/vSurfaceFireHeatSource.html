Heat Source

The heat source is an intermediate value in Rothermel’s surface fire spread model (1972) (p.6, eq. 9). The surface rate of spread is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, the denominator). The propagating heat flux is increased by wind and slope factors due to flame tilting over the potential fuel to get the total propagating heat flux or heat source.
